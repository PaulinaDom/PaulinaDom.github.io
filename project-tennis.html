SQL Analysis · Project 01

ATP Tennis Matches
2000 – 2024

SQL Window Functions CTEs Pivot Tables Data Exploration

A deep-dive into 24 years of professional tennis, exploring who truly dominated the sport — and whether the data supports the GOAT debate. Using a Kaggle ATP dataset of all matches from 2000 to 2024, I set out to find the three greatest players of the era and track the arc of their careers.

The Question

Who dominated tennis in the modern era?

The ATP dataset covers every professional match from 2000 through 2024 — over two decades of data. The goal was to identify the top performers, then drill into the detail: surface preferences, Grand Slam accumulation, and signs of career decline.

One data quality issue I caught and fixed before analysis: a missing US Open 2019 final row that other analysts had flagged on Kaggle. I manually corrected this in Excel before importing — a small detail that reflects the real-world messiness of data work.

Key Findings

What the data revealed

24 Years of data
3 Undisputed GOATs
16 Grand Slam gap between 3rd and 4th place
01 Djokovic, Nadal, and Federer are in a class of their own — the gap between 3rd and 4th place in Grand Slam titles is a staggering 16 titles, confirming their dominance statistically.
02 Surface specialization matters. Andy Murray ranks top-5 on grass and hard, but drops to 56th on clay. David Ferrer excels on clay (2nd) but falls off on grass (40th). The Big Three never fall outside the top 9 on any surface.
03 Federer had a 12-title head start. A rolling sum of Grand Slam wins shows that when Djokovic won his first Australian Open in 2008, Federer had already accumulated 12 titles — making Djokovic's eventual overtake even more remarkable.
SQL Techniques

Selected queries

Below are two queries that illustrate the kind of SQL used throughout this project — from window functions to CTEs with date calculations.

Surface dominance ranking — CTE + Window Function SQL 
-- Setting up a CTE that filters surface, groups by surface
-- and winner, counts wins, then ranks within each surface
WITH wins_surface AS (
    SELECT Surface, Winner, COUNT(*) AS count
    FROM 'atp_tennis.csv'
    WHERE Surface IN ('Clay', 'Grass', 'Hard')
    GROUP BY Surface, Winner),

wins_surface_ranked AS (
    SELECT Surface, Winner,
        RANK() OVER(PARTITION BY Surface ORDER BY count DESC) AS rank_within_surface,
        count
    FROM wins_surface)

SELECT Surface, Winner, rank_within_surface
FROM wins_surface_ranked
WHERE Winner IN ('Federer R.', 'Nadal R.', 'Djokovic N.', 'Murray A.', 'Ferrer D.');
Days since last ATP title — CTE + Date arithmetic SQL 
-- CTE holds the date of each player's most recent ATP title
-- then inner join back to calculate elapsed days
WITH last_atp_titles AS (
    SELECT Winner, MAX(Date) AS last_ATP_title
    FROM 'atp_tennis.csv'
    WHERE Winner IN ('Federer R.', 'Nadal R.', 'Djokovic N.')
        AND Round = 'The Final'
    GROUP BY Winner)

SELECT atp.Winner,
    last_atp_titles.last_atp_title,
    (now()::date - Date::date) AS days_since_last_won_tournament,
    atp.Tournament, atp.Player_1, atp.Player_2
FROM 'atp_tennis.csv' AS atp
INNER JOIN last_atp_titles
    ON last_atp_titles.last_ATP_title = atp.Date
        AND last_atp_titles.Winner = atp.Winner
ORDER BY days_since_last_won_tournament ASC;
