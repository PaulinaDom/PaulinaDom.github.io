Power BI Power Query DAX Data Cleaning Healthcare Data

An interactive 6-page Power BI report exploring how age, gender, BMI, smoking status, and family size drive health insurance charges — turning a raw Kaggle dataset into a navigable analytical story.

The Question

What drives insurance charges?

The dataset, sourced from Kaggle, contains 1,338 health insurance records with attributes including age, sex, BMI, number of children, smoking status, region, and total charges. The goal was to build a comprehensive patient profile overview and identify which factors most significantly impact what people pay.

Data was prepared in Power Query — column profiling, index columns, and custom BMI and age bins were added before any modelling began.

01Smoking is the dominant cost driver. Smokers represent only 20.5% of patients but account for 49% of all charges. Average smoker charges are 4× higher; median charges nearly 5× higher than non-smokers.
02Charges rise consistently with age across average, median, and total measures — a clear linear trend visible across all pages.
0381.9% of patients are overweight, rising by ~10 percentage points with age. Overweight patients contribute to 86% of total charges.
04Patients with 1–3 children tend to have the highest insurance charges — possibly reflecting family coverage and broader risk profiles.
05Scatter plots confirm that smokers sit in a consistently higher charge band than non-smokers at every BMI level, ruling out BMI as the primary explanation.
Report Design

6 interactive pages

The report uses arrow navigation buttons in the top-right corner of each page for clean UX. Slicers and cross-filtering enable dynamic exploration throughout.

Bar & Column Charts
Age group comparisons, charge breakdowns by smoking status, BMI category, and region.
Line & Combo Charts
Charge trends by age with overlaid averages and medians for clear longitudinal reading.
Scatter Plots
Smoker vs. non-smoker charge distribution with BMI on one axis — confirming smoking as primary driver.
Pie Charts & Cards
Gender balance, smoking share, and high-level KPI cards for at-a-glance context.
Tables
Detailed record views with conditional formatting to surface outliers.
Slicers & Nav Buttons
Dynamic filtering and page navigation enabling multi-dimensional self-service exploration.
DAX Measures

Selected measures

Average charges for non-smokers — CALCULATE()DAX
-- Using CALCULATE to filter context before aggregating
Avg Charges Non-Smokers =
CALCULATE(
    AVERAGE(insurance[charges]),
    insurance[smoker] = "no"
)
Count of patients by BMI weight categoryDAX
-- BMI bins created in Power Query; counted here per category
Count Overweight =
CALCULATE(
    COUNTROWS(insurance),
    insurance[bmi_category] = "Overweight"
)
